HOW ASME STARTED?

ASME was founded in 1880 to provide a setting for engineers to discuss the concerns brought by the rise of industrialization and mechanization. The Society’s founders were some of the more prominent machine builders and technical innovators of the late nineteenth century; led by prominent steel engineer Alexander Lyman Holley, Henry Rossiter Worthington and John Edison Sweet.

American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME)/Founders

  • Alexander Lyman Holley
  • Henry Rossiter Worthington
  • John Edson Sweet
  • Matthias N. Forney

MAIN PURPOSE OF ASME

The American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) is a professional association that, in its own words, "promotes the art, science, and practice of multidisciplinary engineering and allied sciences around the globe" via "continuing education, training and professional development, codes and standards, research, conferences and publications, government relations, and other forms of outreach." ASME is thus an engineering society, a standards organization, a research and development organization, a lobbying organization, a provider of training and education, and a nonprofit organization. Founded as an engineering society focused on mechanical engineering in North America, ASME is today multidisciplinary and global.

ASME IN GLA UNIVERSITY

ASME GLA University Student Section is chartered at mechanical engineering department of the university with the aim to complement the curriculum of the university, to offer mutual support in study, learning, and professionalism; to provide personal connections and communications within the Society and the profession and to promote goals of the society. The Section has organized and provide a variety of activities of technical content and professional and personal value such as technical presentations; professional introductions and relationships; engineering and technical Competitions, engineering-related volunteer and community services etc. Society is open for every student without any kind of discrimination.

ASME Student Section of GLA University, Mathura was inaugurated on 26th September 2014 by Mr. Madhukar Sharma (President of ASME India) by lighting the lamp. In his inaugural address he said “Through ASME, students can attain international relationships, they can prosper in their education & profession.” He also appealed youth of today to commit themselves towards eliminating problems in the society through engineering solutions”.
